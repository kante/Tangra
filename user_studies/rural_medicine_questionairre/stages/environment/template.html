Physical Environment

Background Information

Air, water, and land quality are essential for good health as they provide a vehicle for pathogen and carcinogen transport to humans. While exposure to many of these pathogens and carcinogens is infrequent, appropriate monitoring of the physical environments quality is essential to public health maintenance. This is especially meaningful to agricultural communities, where some residents may use pesticides.

Goals

  • Adequate monitoring of air, water, and land quality
  • Reduction of auto-dependent environment
  • Promotion of sound protection and time limits for noise pollution

References

  • Gray, J.A., Zimmerman, J.L., & Rimmer, J.H. (2012). Built environment instruments for walkability, bikeability, and recreation: Disability and universal design relevant? Disability and Health Journal, 5, 87-101.
