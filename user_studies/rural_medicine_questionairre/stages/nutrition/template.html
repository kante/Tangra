Nutrition and Food Security

Background Information

Healthy eating is fundamental to good health. A principal contributor to overweight and obesity is consumption of high-caloric foods which is typical of “fast food” and junk foods. The concept of “food desert,” decreased access to nutritious food, has emerged as a consequence of rural food sources. For example, in rural areas, community members often travel between 25-50km to access grocery stores. This can make fresh and healthy food inconvenient, time consuming, and expensive options.

Goals

  • Improved access to community supermarkets, farmer’s markets, and community gardens
  • Increase availability of healthier foods and beverages in public service venues (schools, after-school programs, child care centers, community recreational facilities, city and county buildings, prisons, juvenile detention centers)

References
